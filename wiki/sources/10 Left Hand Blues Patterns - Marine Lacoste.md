# 10 Left Hand Blues Patterns — Marine Lacoste

**Format:** PDF (piano method with notation)
**Author/Teacher:** Marine Lacoste
**Core focus:** Ten left-hand patterns for blues piano, covering the 12-bar blues form, walking bass lines, and blues-specific rhythmic/harmonic patterns

## Key takeaways

- **12-bar blues form** is the structural backbone of blues and much jazz:
  ```
  | I  | I  | I  | I  |
  | IV | IV | I  | I  |
  | V  | IV | I  | I (turnaround) |
  ```
- **Left-hand pattern variety** is key to blues comping:
  1. Root only (whole notes)
  2. Root + 5th (octave bass)
  3. Root–5th–6th pattern (I–V–VI swing cell)
  4. Root–5th–b7–octave (dominant feel)
  5. Boogie-woogie bass (root + alternating 5th/6th)
  6. Walking bass (quarter-note single notes connecting chord tones)
  7. Chromatic approach from bIII to III
  8. Shuffle pattern (triplet feel)
  9. Broken octave bass
  10. Full chord comping LH
- **Walking bass** connects chord tones with stepwise and chromatic motion: I → III → V → VI → [next chord root]. Essential skill for jazz as well as blues.
- The chromatic bIII→III approach (e.g., E♭→E in C blues) is a defining sound of blues and bebop.
