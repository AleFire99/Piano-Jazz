# Blues Scales Reference

**Format:** PDF ×2 (minor blues scale + major blues scale, all 12 keys)
**Author/Teacher:** Unknown (reference sheets)
**Core focus:** Minor and major blues scales notated in all 12 keys

## Key takeaways

- **Minor blues scale**: 6 notes — root, ♭3, 4, ♭5, 5, ♭7. The ♭5 is the "blue note" — the defining dissonance of the blues sound.
  - Example in C: C – E♭ – F – G♭ – G – B♭
- **Major blues scale**: 6 notes — root, 2, ♭3, 3, 5, 6. Contains both the minor 3rd (♭3) and major 3rd (3) together — the clash that gives the blues its emotional ambiguity.
  - Example in C: C – D – E♭ – E – G – A
- Both scales are pentatonic extensions: minor blues = minor pentatonic + ♭5; major blues = major pentatonic + ♭3.
- The two scales can be used interchangeably over a blues in the same key for color.
