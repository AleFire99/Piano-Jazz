# 29 Sweet Jazz Piano Licks — Julian Bradley

**Format:** PDF (jazz piano lesson book)
**Author/Teacher:** Julian Bradley
**Core focus:** 29 ready-to-use lick patterns with explanations of underlying improvisation techniques: approach notes, enclosure, scale choice, sequences, and in/out playing

## Key takeaways

- **Scale choice per chord function** is the foundational map for improvisation:
  - ii chord → Dorian mode or bebop scale
  - V7 chord → Major scale, diminished scale, or altered scale
  - I chord → Major scale or Dorian
  - Minor ii (ø) → Locrian → resolve to altered → resolve to Dorian
- **Half-step below approach** (chromatic lower neighbor): play the note one half-step below your target, then step up. The most common single-note approach.
- **Chromatic enclosure**: surround the target note from both above and below before landing on it (e.g., target C → play D♭, then B, then C).
- **Sequences**: take a melodic shape and repeat it at different pitch levels (ascending or descending). Creates momentum and logic in improvised lines.
- **IN/OUT playing**: start a phrase in the key (IN), briefly move outside using altered or diminished material (OUT), then resolve back into the key (IN). Creates tension and release.
