# 23 Sweet Chord Progressions & Licks — Julian Bradley

**Format:** PDF (jazz piano lesson book)
**Author/Teacher:** Julian Bradley
**Core focus:** 23 reusable jazz chord progressions and lick patterns, with voicing applications and harmonic explanations

## Key takeaways

- **Tritone substitution** is one of the most powerful reharmonization tools: any dominant chord can be replaced by the dominant a tritone away (G7 → D♭7). Both share the same tritone (B/F = C♭/F).
- **Glide progressions** create smooth voice leading: two voices move by half-step, two voices stay static — produces a floating, cinematic sound.
- **The Never-Ending Progression** cycles through all 12 keys: min7 → maj7 (up a half-step) → min7 (up a major 3rd), repeat.
- **Block chords** thicken a melody by doubling it in octaves and inserting chord tones in between.
- **Montuno** is the Cuban salsa syncopation pattern for piano: short rhythmic cells looped over static or moving harmony.
