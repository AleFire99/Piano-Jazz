## **Tips for Practicing Scales**
1. Use a **metronome** for consistent timing.  
2. Start at a slow tempo, gradually increasing speed.  
3. Focus on even tone and rhythm across all fingers.  
4. Divide practice:  
   - Play hands separately first, then together.  
   - Alternate between rhythms and triads.  

Mastering scales ensures a solid technical base, facilitating your growth as a jazz pianist.

---

### **1. Building the Scales**

- **Monthly Focus**:  
  Each month emphasizes one **major key** and its **minor key** (not necessarily the relative minor).  

- **Scale Formulas**:  
  - **Major Scale Formula**: Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Whole-Half (W-W-H-W-W-W-H).  
  - **Natural Minor Scale Formula**: Whole-Half-Whole-Whole-Half-Whole-Whole (W-H-W-W-H-W-W).  

- **Chromatic Scale**:  
  Includes all 12 semitones (half-steps).  

- **Diatonic Scale**:  
  Any heptatonic scale with 5 whole steps and 2 half-steps.  

- **Fingering Techniques**:  
  Proper fingering is emphasized to ensure smooth and consistent play.

---

### **2. Playing Techniques**
To enhance rhythm, fluency, and harmony:

- **Charleston Rhythm**:  
  A syncopated rhythm (1-2-&-3-4-&) introduces swing to your scales.

- **Diatonic Triads**:  
  Play triads derived from the scale to build understanding of harmony.  

- **Hands Together**:  
  Practice scales with both hands in unison to develop coordination.

- **Two Octaves**:  
  Expand scale practice across two octaves to improve flexibility.
