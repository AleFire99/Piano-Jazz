# **Beginner Plan**

The goal of this plan is to help you build a solid foundation in music theory, technique, and improvisation. As a beginner, you'll focus on basic concepts and gradually build your skills across all five pillars.

---

### **1. Monthly Key Focus**
Each month, you'll focus on one major key and its relative minor. This will help you:

- Learn the basic scales in each key.
- Familiarize yourself with the major and minor chords in those keys.
- Build muscle memory and confidence in all 12 keys.

#### **Example:**
- **Month 1**: C major & A minor
- **Month 2**: G major & E minor
- **Month 3**: D major & B minor

---

### **2. The Five Pillars**

#### **1. [[Pillar - Scales]]**
- **Goal**: Develop basic finger dexterity and understanding of major and minor scales.
- **Tasks**:
  - Practice 5-finger positions for each key.
  - Play simple major and minor scales hands separately.
  - Begin practicing scales hands together at a slow tempo.
- [[Beginner scales exercises]]

#### **2. [[Pillar - Chords]]**
- **Goal**: Learn the basic building blocks of harmony and chord progressions.
- **Tasks**:
  - Learn major and minor triads in all keys.
  - Practice chord inversions.
  - Start working on basic progressions like I-IV-V (C-F-G in C major).
-  [[Beginner chords exercises]]
#### **3. [[Pillar - Voicings]]**
- **Goal**: Start building simple chord voicings.
- **Tasks**:
  - Learn basic triad voicings (root position, first inversion, second inversion).
  - Experiment with adding simple voicings to your chord progressions (I-IV-V).
  - Play chords in different inversions.
-  [[Beginner voicings exercises]]

#### **4. [[Pillar - Lead Sheets]]**
- **Goal**: Combine melody and harmony to play simple songs.
- **Tasks**:
  - Start playing basic lead sheets that feature simple melodies and chord symbols.
  - Focus on understanding how the melody fits with the harmony.
  - Practice reading chord symbols and playing the appropriate chords.
- [[Beginner lead sheets exercises]]
#### **5. [[Pillar - Improvisation]]**
- **Goal**: Begin experimenting with creating your own simple melodies.
- **Tasks**:
  - Improvise using the major and minor scales you've learned.
  - Focus on basic rhythmic patterns and note choices.
  - Use chord progressions like I-IV-V as a foundation for your improvisation.
-  [[Beginner improvisation exercises]]

---

### **3. Weekly Practice Breakdown**

- **Monday**: Scale practice (hands separately, then hands together)
- **Tuesday**: Chord practice (triads and inversions)
- **Wednesday**: Lead sheets (simple songs, focus on melody and harmony)
- **Thursday**: Voicings practice (chord inversions and voicings)
- **Friday**: Improvisation (improv over basic progressions)
- **Saturday**: Review and practice any challenging areas
- **Sunday**: Rest or light practice (review scales and chords)

---

### **4. Blues Integration (add when ready)**

Once you're comfortable with basic scales and chords, begin incorporating blues material:

#### Blues checklist
- [ ] Learn the [[12-Bar Blues]] form in C (I7 / IV7 / V7)
- [ ] Play the C minor blues scale (C E♭ F G♭ G B♭) hands separately
- [ ] Play the C major blues scale (C D E♭ E G A) hands separately
- [ ] Practice LH Pattern 3 (root–5th–6th swing cell) over the 12-bar form
- [ ] Add walking bass (LH Pattern 6) connecting chord tones

See [[Blues Scale]] and [[12-Bar Blues]] for full reference.

#### Lick vocabulary checklist
- [ ] Practice one half-step-below approach into a chord tone (see [[Approach Notes]])
- [ ] Practice a chromatic enclosure into the 3rd of Cmaj7
- [ ] Take a 3-note melodic shape and sequence it up by step
- [ ] Play a simple IN/OUT phrase: 2 bars in the key, 1 bar outside (altered), resolve

---

### **5. Progress Tracking**

Keep track of your progress each week:

- Are you able to play your scales at a steady tempo?
- Can you play major and minor triads in all keys?
- Are you comfortable reading lead sheets with basic chord symbols?
- Can you play through a 12-bar blues form with a left-hand pattern?

---

By the end of the first few months, you should be able to confidently play basic scales, chords, and lead sheets, and begin experimenting with simple improvisation. This plan is meant to give you the foundation for more advanced concepts down the road. Stay consistent and patient with your practice!
