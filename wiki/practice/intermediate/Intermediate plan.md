# **Intermediate Plan**

This plan is designed for musicians who have a basic understanding of scales, chords, and simple songs. At this level, you'll deepen your knowledge of music theory, develop technical skills, and expand your improvisation abilities. The focus will be on enhancing your playing, learning more complex progressions, and incorporating more advanced concepts into your improvisation.

---

### **1. Monthly Key Focus**
Each month, you'll focus on a new major key and its relative minor, along with key signatures and modes. This will help you:

- Learn more advanced scale patterns, including [[Modes|modes]].
- Deepen your understanding of chord progressions and harmony.
- Become comfortable with more complex rhythms and chord changes.

#### **Example:**
- **Month 1**: C major & A minor, explore modes (Ionian, Dorian, Phrygian)
- **Month 2**: G major & E minor, explore secondary dominants
- **Month 3**: D major & B minor, work on diminished and augmented chords

---

### **2. The Five Pillars**

#### **1. [[Pillar - Scales]]**
- **Goal**: Expand your scale knowledge to include modes and other scale types.
- **Tasks**:
  - Learn modes (Dorian, Phrygian, Lydian, Mixolydian) in all keys.
  - Practice scale patterns in both hands, focusing on smooth transitions.
  - Start practicing two-octave scales and arpeggios for each mode.

#### **2. [[Pillar - Chords]]**
- **Goal**: Deepen your knowledge of harmony with more complex chords and progressions.
- **Tasks**:
  - Learn seventh chords (major, minor, dominant) and inversions.
  - Start learning extended chords (9ths, 11ths, 13ths).
  - Practice common chord progressions, including ii-V-I in various keys.

#### **3. [[Pillar - Voicings]]**
- **Goal**: Create more jazz-style harmonizations and chord voicings.
- **Tasks**:
  - Practice rootless voicings (especially for dominant seventh chords).
  - Experiment with drop 2 and drop 3 voicings.
  - Practice voice leading techniques to create smoother transitions between chords.

#### **4. [[Pillar - Lead Sheets]]**
- **Goal**: Enhance your ability to play more complex lead sheets with different harmonies.
- **Tasks**:
  - Practice lead sheets with more complex chord progressions and inversions.
  - Learn to play both the melody and harmony while reading the sheet music.
  - Focus on reading more complex rhythms and syncopations.

#### **5. [[Pillar - Improvisation]]**
- **Goal**: Expand your improvisation skills by incorporating more advanced techniques and harmonic knowledge.
- **Tasks**:
  - Improvise using modes and seventh chords.
  - Start incorporating chromaticism and passing tones in your improvisation.
  - Improvise over more complex progressions (ii-V-I, iii-vi-ii-V).
  - Focus on rhythmic variety, experimenting with different subdivisions of beats.

---

### **3. Weekly Practice Breakdown**

- **Monday**: Scales and arpeggios (hands together, two octaves)
- **Tuesday**: Chord practice (seventh chords, inversions, and extended chords)
- **Wednesday**: Lead sheets (focus on more complex harmonies and rhythms)
- **Thursday**: Voicings (rootless voicings, drop 2, and drop 3 voicings)
- **Friday**: Improvisation (over ii-V-I progressions and modal improvisation)
- **Saturday**: Review and practice challenging areas (focus on weaker skills)
- **Sunday**: Rest or light practice (review scales and chord progressions)

---

### **4. Progress Tracking**

Monitor your progress each week:

- Are you able to play scales and arpeggios fluently across multiple octaves?
- Can you play seventh chords and extended chords in all keys?
- Are you able to read and perform more complex lead sheets?
- How comfortable are you improvising with different chord progressions and modes?

---

By the end of this plan, you should be able to confidently play and improvise over more complex chord progressions and modal scales, and understand a broader range of harmony and voice leading. This intermediate plan will build the foundation for even more advanced jazz theory and performance skills in the future. Keep practicing consistently to improve your technique and musicality!
