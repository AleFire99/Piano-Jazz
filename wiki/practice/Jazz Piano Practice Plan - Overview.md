Welcome to your **1-Year Jazz Piano Practice Plan**! This structured plan is designed to help you develop foundational skills in jazz piano by focusing on key areas of practice, referred to as the **Five Pillars**. Each month, you will explore a new major key and its relative minor, ensuring comprehensive growth in your jazz piano journey.

The notes follows this [guide](https://pianowithjonny.com/piano-lessons/jazz-piano-1-year-practice-plan/#).

## **Plan Structure**

### **1. Monthly Key Focus**
Each month, you will focus on one major key and its relative minor. This allows you to:  
- Gain confidence in all 12 keys.  
- Build muscle memory for scales, chords, and improvisation patterns.  

### **2. The Five Pillars**
The plan is divided into five main areas of practice:  
1. **[[Pillar - Scales]]**: Develop finger dexterity and familiarity with the keyboard.  
2. **[[Pillar - Chords]]**: Learn the building blocks of jazz harmony.  
3. **[[Pillar - Voicings]]**: Create jazz-style harmonizations.  
4. **[[Pillar - Lead Sheets]]**: Combine melody and harmony to play complete songs.  
5. **[[Pillar - Improvisation]]**: Experiment with creating your own melodies.

### **3. Difficulty**
Each section of this plan will vary in difficulty based on your experience and progress. To ensure you focus on the right material for your skill level, reference the difficulty levels below.  
You can access detailed documents for each difficulty level:  

- **[[Beginner plan|Beginner difficulty]]**: Aimed at those new to music or transitioning from basic to intermediate skills.
- **[[Intermediate plan|Intermediate difficulty]]**: For players who have a good understanding of music theory, scales, and basic improvisation.
- **[[Advanced plan|Advanced difficulty]]**: For experienced musicians looking to master advanced techniques and complex harmonic concepts.


---

## **Benefits of This Plan**
By following this practice plan, you will:  
- Master the foundational elements of jazz piano.  
- Improve your ability to play jazz standards fluently.  
- Develop improvisational skills for creative expression.  
- Gain confidence in reading and interpreting lead sheets.  
- Build a strong technical foundation for advanced jazz concepts.

---

## **How to Use This Plan**
1. **Daily Practice**: Dedicate time to each of the five pillars every day. Even 20-30 minutes per day can lead to significant progress.  
2. **Sequential Progression**: Move through the keys in the order provided, ensuring a balanced and thorough learning experience.  
3. **Focus on Consistency**: Aim for steady, consistent practice rather than long, irregular sessions.  

---

## **What You’ll Need**
- A piano or keyboard with at least 61 keys.  
- A metronome (physical or digital).  
- Beginner-friendly lead sheets for jazz standards like “Autumn Leaves.”  
- A positive attitude and patience with yourself as you learn!
