## Big picture

Jazz piano mastery is built on five interlocking pillars. They are not independent — each reinforces the others:

- **Scales** provide the tonal vocabulary for improvisation and help you navigate keys.
- **Chords** define the harmonic language and are the foundation of comping and voice leading.
- **Voicings** are how chords are realized on the piano — the same chord can sound thin or rich depending on voicing.
- **Lead Sheets** translate the written jazz language (chord symbols + melody) into a performance.
- **Improvisation** synthesizes all four above into spontaneous melodic expression.

The ii-V-I progression is the central building block of jazz harmony. Nearly everything else is a variation, extension, or reharmonization of it.

## Improvisation picture (established)

The improvisation domain now has a clear framework:

1. **Chord tones** first — target the 3rd and 7th of each chord
2. **Scale choice map** — Dorian/bebop over ii; Mixolydian/altered/diminished over V7; major/Dorian over I
3. **Approach notes** — half-step below or chromatic enclosure into any chord tone
4. **Bebop scale** — aligns chord tones to strong beats via one added chromatic note
5. **Sequences** — repeat a shape at different pitch levels for linear momentum
6. **IN/OUT** — deliberate outside playing for tension, always resolved back

The [[Altered Dominants]] + [[Tritone Substitution]] pages complete the harmonic reharmonization vocabulary at the intermediate level.
