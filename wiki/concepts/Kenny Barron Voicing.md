# Kenny Barron Voicing

A two-handed chord voicing built from **stacks of 5ths**. Named after jazz pianist Kenny Barron — hear it at the opening of his composition *Spiral*. One of the few jazz voicings that genuinely requires large hands (you need to stretch a 9th in each hand).

---

## Structure

Both hands build stacks of 5ths. The left hand starts from the root; the right hand starts from the chord's 3rd:

- **Left hand:** root → up a 5th → up another 5th (1 – 5 – 9)
- **Right hand:** 3rd → up a 5th → up another 5th (3 – 7 – 11)

### C minor 11 example:
- LH: C – G – D (1 – 5 – 9)
- RH: Eb – Bb – F (3 – 7 – 11)
- Full voicing: **(1 5 9 / 3 7 11)**

### C major 7 #11 example (shift right hand up a half step):
- LH: C – G – D (1 – 5 – 9)
- RH: E – B – F# (3 – 7 – #11)
- Full voicing: **(1 5 9 / 3 7 #11)**

---

## Suggested use (Melody-Matching)

| Chord type | Ideal when melody is |
|------------|----------------------|
| Minor 7 | 11th |
| Major 7 | #11th |

The voicing is designed so the melody note is already built into the top of the right hand — just play the voicing and the chord + melody sound together.

---

## Sawn-off variation (for smaller hands)

If you can't stretch a 9th, cut off the top note of the left hand:
- LH: root + 5th only (1 – 5)
- RH: 9th – 3rd – 7th (9 / 3 7)
- Full: **(1 5 / 9 3 7)** — e.g., C minor 7: LH = C G, RH = D Eb Bb

This works best when the melody is the **7th** (Bb for C minor 7).
