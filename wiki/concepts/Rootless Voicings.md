# Rootless Voicings

Julian Bradley's most-used jazz piano voicing. Rootless voicings omit the root and are built upward from the chord's **3rd** — this allows you to reach the 9th, adding a colorful, sophisticated sound.

---

## Why rootless?

- Designed for playing **with a bass player**: the bassist handles the root, freeing both your hands for melody and harmony
- For **solo piano**: play the root in your left hand as a bass note, rootless voicing in your right hand
- Rootless voicings are **stacks of 3rds starting from the 3rd**: 3 – 5 – 7 – 9

---

## Two positions (like [[Shell Voicings]])

**Position A** — built from the 3rd upward:

| Chord | Notes (bottom → top) | Degrees |
|-------|----------------------|---------|
| C major 7 | E G B D | 3 5 7 9 |
| C minor 7 | Eb G Bb D | 3 5 7 9 |
| C7 | E G Bb D | 3 5 7 9 |

*Shortcut for C major 7:* Find the major 3rd (E), then play a **minor 7 chord** from that note (E minor 7 = E G B D).
*Shortcut for C minor 7:* Find the minor 3rd (Eb), then play a **major 7 chord** from that note (Eb major 7 = Eb G Bb D).

**Special case for V7 chords:** Replace the 5th with the **6th (13th)**:

| Chord | Standard pos A | V7 variation |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| C7 | E G Bb D (3 5 7 9) | E A Bb D (3 6 7 9) |

**Position B** — Position A in **2nd inversion** (drop the top two notes down an octave):

| Chord | Notes (bottom → top) | Degrees |
|-------|----------------------|---------|
| C major 7 | B D E G | 7 9 3 5 |
| C minor 7 | Bb D Eb G | 7 9 3 5 |
| C7 | Bb D E A | 7 9 3 6 |

*Position B is the same four notes as Position A — just inverted so 7 and 9 are at the bottom.*

---

## Suggested use (Melody-Matching)

| Melody note | Use |
|-------------|-----|
| 9th | Position A (top note = 9th) |
| 5th | Position B (top note = 5th) |

For solo piano: root in left hand + rootless voicing in right hand = melody note on top.
For band: rootless voicing in left hand, melody or solo in right hand.

---

## Lead sheet note

Most lead sheets write chords as 7th chords (Cm7, G7, etc.) even when extensions are implied. You can freely add the 9th to any 7th chord — you don't need the sheet to say "Cm9" to play the 9th.
