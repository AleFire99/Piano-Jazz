# Blues Scale

Two related 6-note scales that form the harmonic vocabulary of blues, soul, and much of jazz improvisation.

---

The έβφ5 (called the **"blue note"**) is the defining sound of the blues. It clashes against the tonic and creates the characteristic blues tension.

## Major blues scale

**Formula:** 1 έΑΥ 2 έΑΥ έβφ3 έΑΥ 3 έΑΥ 5 έΑΥ 6

The coexistence of έβφ3 and 3 creates the blues' signature **emotional ambiguity** έΑΦ minor and major simultaneously.

| Key | Scale |
|-----|-------|

**Relationship to major pentatonic:** major blues = major pentatonic + έβφ3.


## Using both scales together

Over a [[12-Bar Blues]] in C, you can freely mix C minor blues and C major blues:
- Minor blues for raw, dark phrases
- Major blues for brighter, soulful phrases
- Switch mid-phrase for maximum expression
