# Pillar — Lead Sheets

A lead sheet gives you the melody (standard notation) and chord symbols. Everything else — harmony, rhythm, arrangement — is your interpretation. Reading lead sheets fluently is the gateway to the jazz repertoire.

---

## The two skills

**1. Reading chord symbols** — knowing what every symbol means (see [[Chord Symbols]] for the full reference)

**2. Voicing the chords** — translating symbols into actual notes on the piano using your voicing toolkit (see [[Melody-Matching]] for the technique)

---

## Chord symbol quick reference

| Symbol type | Examples | What it means |
|-------------|----------|---------------|
| Major 7 | Cmaj7, CΔ, C^ | C E G B |
| Minor 7 | Cm7, C-7 | C Eb G Bb |
| Dominant 7 | C7 | C E G Bb |
| Minor-major 7 | CmΔ | C Eb G B |
| Half-diminished | Cø, Cm7b5 | C Eb Gb Bb |
| Diminished 7 | C°7 | C Eb Gb A |
| 6th chord | C6, Cm6 | 6th replaces 7th; Cm6 = A natural (not Ab) |
| Suspended 4th | C7sus4 | 4th replaces 3rd |
| Extensions | C9, Cm11, C13 | Built from major scale |
| Altered | C7(b9), C7(#9), C7alt | Altered extensions on V7 only |
| Slash chord | Bb/C | Bb chord, C in bass |

Full reference: [[Chord Symbols]]. For altered dominants specifically: [[Altered Dominants]].

---

## How to play from a lead sheet

1. **Identify the chord symbol** — what chord type and extensions?
2. **Look at the melody note** — what scale degree is it relative to the chord?
3. **Choose a voicing** — use [[Melody-Matching]] to pick the voicing whose top note is the melody note
4. **Play chord and melody together** — the melody sits on top of the voicing

As you move through a song, alternate voicing types naturally (shells, open, rootless, etc.) for variety.

---

## The Real Book

Most jazz standards are published in the Real Book — a collection of lead sheets. The notation uses the chord symbols covered in [[Chord Symbols]]. Standards are written in 4/4, 3/4, or occasionally 5/4. The head (melody) is played once through, then soloists improvise over the chord changes, then the head is repeated.
