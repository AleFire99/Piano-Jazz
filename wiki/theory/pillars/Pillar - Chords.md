# Pillar — Chords

Chords are the harmonic backbone of jazz. Knowing how they're built and how they move gives you the foundation for comping, improvisation, and arrangement.

## 7th chords

The six chord types that matter in jazz:

| Name | Symbol | Notes (C) | Semitones | Role |
|------|--------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Major 7 | Cmaj7, CΔ | C E G B | 4,3,4 | I of major ii-V-I |
| Minor 7 | Cm7 | C Eb G Bb | 3,4,3 | ii of major ii-V-I |
| Dominant 7 | C7 | C E G Bb | 4,3,3 | V of any ii-V-I |
| Minor-major 7 | CmΔ | C Eb G B | 3,4,4 | I of minor ii-V-I |
| Half-diminished | Cø, Cm7b5 | C Eb Gb Bb | 3,3,4 | ii of minor ii-V-I |
| Diminished 7 | C°7 | C Eb Gb A | 3,3,3 | Passing/substitute |

For full symbol notation (including extensions, alterations, slash chords): [[Chord Symbols]].

## Extensions

Extensions are built from the chord's **major scale** — count up 9, 11, or 13 from the root. They can be added to any 7th chord:

- **9th** — adds color (Dm9, Cmaj9, G9)
- **11th** — adds tension; use `#4` notation for maj7 chords, `11` for min7/V7
- **13th** — only V7 chords use "13" symbol (maj/min chords call it the "6th")
- **Altered extensions** (b9, #9, #11, b13) — V7 chords only; see [[Altered Dominants]]

---

## Core progressions

**ii-V-I (major):** Dm7 — G7 — Cmaj7
**ii-V-I (minor):** Dø — G7(b9) — Cm
**I-IV-V (blues):** C7 — F7 — G7
**iii-VI-ii-V:** Em7 — A7 — Dm7 — G7

The ii-V-I is the single most important progression in jazz. Almost every standard is built from chains of ii-V-I progressions.
