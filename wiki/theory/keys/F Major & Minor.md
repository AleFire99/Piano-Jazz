## **Pillars for F Major & Minor**

---

### **Scales**

The F major and F minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

#### F Major Scale

F G A Bb C D E F F G A Bb C D E F

- The F major scale is the foundation of Ionian (major mode) and serves as the starting point for F Lydian and other scales.
- The key features include a natural major 7th and a whole-step between most notes.

#### F Minor Scales

- **F Natural Minor (Aeolian)**: F G Ab Bb C Db Eb F E F G  
  - This scale is based on the Aeolian mode and has a natural minor sound with a flat 3rd, 6th, and 7th.

- **F Harmonic Minor**: F G Ab Bb C Db E F E F G  
  - This scale has a raised 7th, creating a leading tone that resolves to the tonic, with a characteristic exotic sound.

- **F Melodic Minor (Jazz)**: F G Ab Bb C D E F G F G  
  - The melodic minor scale, in jazz, is played with a raised 6th and 7th ascending, and the natural 6th and 7th descending.

### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **F major**, start by using basic triads (F A C), and later expand into seventh chords (Fmaj7).
- In **F minor**, use triads (F Ab C) and seventh chords (Fm7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **Fmaj7 (F major)**: F (root), A (third), C (seventh)
- **Fm7 (F minor)**: F (root), Ab (third), C (seventh)
- **G7 (G dominant)**: G (root), B (third), F (seventh)
- **Bbmaj7 (Bb major)**: Bb (root), D (third), F (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In F major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "Fmaj7", "Fm", "G7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "F7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the F major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For F Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (F major), Lydian (C major).
- Use the F major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (F, A, C, E) for Fmaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For F Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the F minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (E) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
