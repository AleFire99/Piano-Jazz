### **Scales**

The D# major and D# minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

#### Dέβψ/Eέβφ Major Scale

D# E F G G# A B C D D# E F G G#

- The D# major scale is the foundation of Ionian (major mode) and also serves as the starting point for D# Lydian and other scales.
- The key features include a natural major 7th and a whole-step between most notes.

#### Dέβψ/Eέβφ Minor Scales

- **D# Natural Minor (Aeolian)**: D# E F# G G# A B C D C# F  
  - This scale is based on the Aeolian mode and has a natural minor sound with a flat 3rd, 6th, and 7th.

- **D# Harmonic Minor**: D# E F# G G# A B C D C# F  
  - This scale has a raised 7th, creating a leading tone that resolves to the tonic, with a characteristic exotic sound.

- **D# Melodic Minor (Jazz)**: D# E F G G# A B C D# C# F  
  - The melodic minor scale, in jazz, is played with a raised 6th and 7th ascending, and the natural 6th and 7th descending.

### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **D# major**, start by using basic triads (D# F# A), and later expand into seventh chords (D#maj7).
- In **D# minor**, use triads (D# F# A) and seventh chords (D#m7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **D#maj7 (D# major)**: D# (root), F# (third), A (seventh)
- **D#m7 (D# minor)**: D# (root), F# (third), B (seventh)
- **E#m7 (E minor)**: E (root), G# (third), B (seventh)
- **A#7 (A# dominant)**: A# (root), C (third), F (seventh)
- **Bmaj7 (B major)**: B (root), D# (third), F# (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In D# major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "D#maj7", "A#m", "G#7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "D#7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the D# major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For D# Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (D# major), Lydian (G# major).
- Use the D# major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (D#, F#, A, C) for D#maj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For D# Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the D# minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (C) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
