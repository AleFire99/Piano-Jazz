## **Pillars for G Major & Minor**

### **Scales**

The G major and G minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

#### G Major Scale

G A B C D E F# G

- The G major scale is the foundation of Ionian (major mode) and serves as the starting point for G Lydian and other scales.
- The key features include a natural major 7th and a whole-step between most notes.

#### G Minor Scales

- **G Natural Minor (Aeolian)**: G A Bb C D Eb F G
  - This scale is based on the Aeolian mode and has a natural minor sound with a flat 3rd, 6th, and 7th.

- **G Harmonic Minor**: G A Bb C D Eb F# G
  - This scale has a raised 7th, creating a leading tone that resolves to the tonic, with a characteristic exotic sound.

- **G Melodic Minor (Jazz)**: G A Bb C D E F# G
  - The melodic minor scale, in jazz, is played with a raised 6th and 7th ascending, and the natural 6th and 7th descending.

### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **G major**, start by using basic triads (G B D), and later expand into seventh chords (Gmaj7).
- In **G minor**, use triads (G Bb D) and seventh chords (Gm7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **Gmaj7 (G major)**: G (root), B (third), D (seventh)
- **Gm7 (G minor)**: G (root), Bb (third), D (seventh)
- **D7 (D dominant)**: D (root), F# (third), C (seventh)
- **Cmaj7 (C major)**: C (root), E (third), G (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In G major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "Gmaj7", "Gm", "D7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "G7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the G major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For G Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (G major), Lydian (D major).
- Use the G major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (G, B, D, F#) for Gmaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For G Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the G minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (F#) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
