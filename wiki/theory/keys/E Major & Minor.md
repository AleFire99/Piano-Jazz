### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **E major**, start by using basic triads (E G‚ôØ B), and later expand into seventh chords (Emaj7).
- In **E minor**, use triads (E G B) and seventh chords (Em7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **Emaj7 (E major)**: E (root), G‚ôØ (third), D‚ôØ (seventh)
- **Em7 (E minor)**: E (root), G (third), D (seventh)
- **F‚ôØm7 (F‚ôØ minor)**: F‚ôØ (root), A (third), E (seventh)
- **G‚ôØm7 (G‚ôØ minor)**: G‚ôØ (root), B (third), F‚ôØ (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In E major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "Emaj7", "Em", "B7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "E7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the E major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For E Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (E major), Lydian (A major).
- Use the E major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (E, G‚ôØ, B, D‚ôØ) for Emaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For E Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the E minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (D‚ôØ) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
