The D major and D minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

#### D Major Scale

### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **D major**, start by using basic triads (D F‚ôØ A), and later expand into seventh chords (Dmaj7).
- In **D minor**, use triads (D F A) and seventh chords (Dm7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **Dmaj7**: D (root), F‚ôØ (third), C‚ôØ (seventh)
- **Dm7**: D (root), F (third), C (seventh)
- **Em7**: E (root), G (third), D (seventh)
- **G7**: G (root), B (third), F (seventh)
- **Am7**: A (root), C (third), G (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In D major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "Dmaj7", "Dm", "G7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "D7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the D major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For D Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (D major), Lydian (G major).
- Use the D major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (D, F‚ôØ, A, C‚ôØ) for Dmaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For D Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the D minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (C‚ôØ) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
