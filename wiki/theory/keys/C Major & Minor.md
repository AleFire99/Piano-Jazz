## **Pillars for C Major & Minor**

---

### **Scales**

The C major and C minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

#### C Major Scale

C D E F G A B C  
- The C major scale is the foundation of Ionian (major mode) and also serves as the starting point for C Lydian and other scales.
- The key features include a natural major 7th and a whole-step between most notes.

#### C Minor Scales

- **C Natural Minor (Aeolian)**: C D♭ E♭ F G♭ A♭ B♭ C  
  - This scale is based on the Aeolian mode and has a natural minor sound with a flat 3rd, 6th, and 7th.

- **C Harmonic Minor**: C D♭ E♭ F G♭ A♭ B C  
  - This scale has a raised 7th, creating a leading tone that resolves to the tonic, with a characteristic exotic sound.

- **C Melodic Minor (Jazz)**: C D E♭ F G A B C  
  - The melodic minor scale, in jazz, is played with a raised 6th and 7th ascending, and the natural 6th and 7th descending.

### **Voicings**

This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

#### Major and Minor Voicings

- For **C major**, start by using basic triads (C E G), and later expand into seventh chords (Cmaj7).
- In **C minor**, use triads (C E♭ G) and seventh chords (Cm7) in root positions and inversions.

#### Open and Closed Voicings

- **Closed voicings** place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
- **Open voicings** spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

#### Shell Voicings

Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

- **Cmaj7 (C major)**: C (root), E (third), B (seventh)
- **Cm7 (C minor)**: C (root), E♭ (third), B♭ (seventh)
- **Dm7 (D minor)**: D (root), F (third), C (seventh)
- **G7 (G dominant)**: G (root), B (third), F (seventh)
- **Am7 (A minor)**: A (root), C (third), G (seventh)

Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both **closed voicing** (notes close together) or **open voicing** (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

### **Lead Sheets**

Lead sheets will help you interpret the harmonies and structure of a song. In C major and minor, you will be able to read chord symbols like "Cmaj7", "Am", "G7", and apply the relevant chord voicings.

#### Key Points:

- Chord symbols indicate which notes to play and often include alterations, extensions (like 7ths, 9ths), or specific modes (such as "C7" for dominant 7th).
- Reading and interpreting these symbols in context is critical for jazz performance.

### **Improvisation**

Improvisation over the C major and minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

#### For C Major:

- **Modes**: Ionian (C major), Lydian (F major).
- Use the C major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (C, E, G, B) for Cmaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

#### For C Minor:

- **Natural Minor**: Use the C minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
- **Harmonic Minor**: Emphasize the raised 7th (B) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
- **Melodic Minor**: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
