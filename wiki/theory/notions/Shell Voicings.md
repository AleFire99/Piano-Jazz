# Shell Voicings

The simplest jazz piano voicing — just three notes: **root, 3rd, and 7th**. These are the three notes that define what kind of chord it is (maj7, min7, or V7). Shell voicings give a bare, minimal sound and are the foundation before adding more complex voicings.

---

## The three notes and what they do

- **Root** — defines the chord's tonal center
- **3rd** — determines major (major 3rd) or minor (minor 3rd)
- **7th** — determines quality: major 7th (maj7), minor 7th (min7 or V7)

The 5th is omitted — it adds no harmonic information and just clutters the voicing.

---

## Two positions (Julian Bradley)

The 3rd and 7th can be arranged in either order above the root:

| Position | Order (bottom → top) | C major 7 | C minor 7 | C7 |
|----------|----------------------|-----------|-----------|----|
| **Position A** | 1 – 3 – 7 | C E B | C Eb Bb | C E Bb |
| **Position B** | 1 – 7 – 3 | C B E | C Bb Eb | C Bb E |

Both positions use the same three notes — only the middle note changes.

### Suggested uses (Melody-Matching)
- When the melody plays the **3rd** → use **Position B** (1 7 3) — 3rd is on top
- When the melody plays the **7th** → use **Position A** (1 3 7) — 7th is on top

See [[Melody-Matching]] for the full system.

---

## Shell voicings for the three main chord types

| Chord type | Position A (1 3 7) | Position B (1 7 3) |
|------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| C major 7 | C E B | C B E |
| C minor 7 | C Eb Bb | C Bb Eb |
| C7 | C E Bb | C Bb E |

## Voice leading in ii-V-I

Shell voicings allow smooth voice leading — notes move by small steps between chords:

```
ii:  Dm7  — D F C    (root, 3rd, 7th)
V:   G7   — G B F    (root, 3rd, 7th)  — F is common tone with Dm7
I:   Cmaj7 — C E B   (root, 3rd, 7th)  — B is a half-step from C
```

The 3rd and 7th of each chord resolve smoothly to the 7th and 3rd of the next (Guide Tones).

---

## When to use shell voicings

- **Minimal, sparse sound** — creates contrast against more complex voicings
- **Songs where melody sits on 3rds and 7ths** (e.g., *All The Things You Are*)
- **Beginner level** — learn these before moving to [[Open Voicings]], [[Rootless Voicings]], [[Kenny Barron Voicing]], and [[Fourth Voicings]]

Shell voicings work especially well juxtaposed with more complex voicings — the contrast is itself an arrangement tool.

## Practice approach

- Learn all 12 keys in Position A, then Position B
- Practice in ii-V-I patterns around the [[Circle of Fifths]]
