    Chord Symbols

    Complete reference for reading chord symbols in jazz lead sheets. Every symbol tells you the chord type and which extensions to add or avoid.

    The six 7th chord types

    These are the core chord types. Learn their symbols, notes, and ii-V-I roles.

    Symbol Name Notes (from C) Role in progressions
    Cmaj7, CΔ, C^ Major 7 C E G B I chord of major ii-V-I
    Cm7, C-7 Minor 7 C Eb G Bb ii chord of major ii-V-I
    C7 Dominant 7 C E G Bb V chord of any ii-V-I
    CmΔ, CmA Minor-major 7 C Eb G B I chord of minor ii-V-I
    Cø, Cm7b5 Half-diminished C Eb Gb Bb ii chord of minor ii-V-I
    C°7 Diminished 7 C Eb Gb A Passing/substitute chord

    The three big ones (maj7, min7, V7) cover the vast majority of jazz standards. The others appear in minor ii-V-I progressions and as passing chords.

    6th chords

    The 6th replaces the 7th (contrast with 13th chords, which add the 6th alongside the 7th).

    Symbol Name Notes (from C) Note
    C6 Major 6 C E G A
    Cm6 Minor 6 C Eb G A ⚠️ Uses major 6th (A), not Ab
    C6/9 Major 6/9 C E G A D
    Cm6/9 Minor 6/9 C Eb G A D

    ⚠️ Common trap: Cm6 has a natural 6th (A), not a flat 6th (Ab). The minor refers to the 3rd, not the 6th.

    9th chords

    Extensions are built from the major scale of the root — count up 9, 11, 13 from C.

    Symbol Notes added Chord type
    Cmaj9 + major 9th (D) maj7 + 9
    Cm9 + major 9th (D) min7 + 9
    C9 + major 9th (D) V7 + 9 (C7 + D)
    CmΔ9 + major 9th (D) minor-major 7 + 9

    Altered 9ths (V7 chords only)

    Symbol Extension Sound
    C7(b9) Flat 9 (Db) Dark, tense
    C7(#9) Sharp 9 (D#/Eb) "James Bond Chord" — bluesy tension

    11th chords

    Notation rule: Use #4 for maj7 chords, use 11 for min7 and V7 chords.

    Symbol Chord Extension
    Cmaj7#4, CΔ#4 Major 7 #4 = raised 11th (F#)
    Cm11 Minor 7 11 = perfect 11th (F)
    C7(#11) Dominant 7 #11 = raised 11th (F#)
    C7(#11/b9) Dominant 7 Both altered extensions

    The 9th is often played along with the 11th even when not notated.

    Suspended 4th chords

    The 4th replaces the 3rd (contrast with 11th chords, where 4th and 3rd coexist).

    • Applied almost exclusively to V7 chords: C7(sus4)
    • Common C7sus4 voicing: Bb F G C (from bottom)

    13th chords

    The 13th is the same pitch as the 6th — one octave higher. Only V7 chords use the symbol "13" (if the chord is major or minor, the same note is called the "6th").

    Symbol Notes Note
    C13 C7 + 6th (A) "C13 is a C7 chord plus a 13th"
    C13(#11) C7 + #11 + 13
    C7(b13) C7 + b13 (Ab) ⚠️ Implies C altered — see Altered Dominants

    The 9th is often included below the 13th even when not notated.

    Slash chords

    Format: Chord/Bass — the chord before the slash goes in the right hand; the bass note after the slash goes in the left hand.

    Symbol Right hand Left hand
    Bb/C Bb chord C
    C/Db C chord Db
    Bbmaj7/C Bbmaj7 chord C

    Slash chords are common for smooth bass lines and pedal-tone effects.

    Quick disambiguation table

    These look similar… But they're different because…
    6th chord vs 13th chord 6th replaces 7th; 13th adds 6th to 7th
    Sus4 chord vs 11th chord Sus4 replaces 3rd; 11th adds 4th to 3rd
    #4 notation vs #11 Same note — #4 is for maj7 chords, #11 for min7/V7
    Cm6 major 6th vs minor 6th Cm6 always uses major 6th (A natural), never Ab
