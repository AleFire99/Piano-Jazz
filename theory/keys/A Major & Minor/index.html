    Pillars for A Major & Minor

    Scales

    The A major and A minor scales form the foundation of this course. Understanding their structure and how they relate to modes is essential to jazz harmony.

    A Major Scale

    A A♯ B C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A
    - The A major scale is the foundation of Ionian (major mode) and also serves as the starting point for Lydian and other scales. - The key features include a natural major 7th and a whole-step between most notes.

    A Minor Scales

    • A Natural Minor (Aeolian): A A♯ B C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A

    • This scale is based on the Aeolian mode and has a natural minor sound with a flat 3rd, 6th, and 7th.

    • A Harmonic Minor: A A♯ B C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A

    • This scale has a raised 7th, creating a leading tone that resolves to the tonic, with a characteristic exotic sound.

    • A Melodic Minor (Jazz): A A♯ B C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A

    • The melodic minor scale, in jazz, is played with a raised 6th and 7th ascending, and the natural 6th and 7th descending.

    Voicings

    This pillar explores how to play the chords in a musically effective and harmonically clear way, including different voicings such as root position, inversions, and shell voicings.

    Major and Minor Voicings

    • For A major, start by using basic triads (A C♯ E), and later expand into seventh chords (Amaj7).
    • In A minor, use triads (A C E) and seventh chords (Am7) in root positions and inversions.

    Open and Closed Voicings

    • Closed voicings place the notes of a chord as close together as possible, creating a tight sound.
    • Open voicings spread the notes across octaves to create a more open, airy sound.

    Shell Voicings

    Shell voicings are an essential component of jazz harmony, where you play only the most essential notes of a chord: the root, third, and seventh. This simplified voicing provides the harmonic context without the clutter of the full chord.

    • Amaj7 (A major): A (root), C♯ (third), G♯ (seventh)
    • Am7 (A minor): A (root), C (third), G (seventh)
    • Bm7 (B minor): B (root), D (third), A (seventh)
    • G7 (G dominant): G (root), B (third), F (seventh)

    Shell voicings are often used in comping and can be played in both closed voicing (notes close together) or open voicing (spread across octaves) to create different textures. These voicings work well when accompanying soloists or for playing rhythmically in an ensemble.

    Improvisation

    Improvisation over the A major and A minor keys can be approached using scales, arpeggios, and chord tones.

    For A Major:

    • Modes: Ionian (A major), Lydian (D major).
    • Use the A major scale for melodic improvisation, focusing on chord tones (A, C♯, E, G♯) for Amaj7, and using appropriate tension notes like the 7th and 9th.

    For A Minor:

    • Natural Minor: Use the A minor scale, focusing on resolving to the tonic.
    • Harmonic Minor: Emphasize the raised 7th (G♯) in improvisation, which creates a strong tension-resolution feel.
    • Melodic Minor: Use ascending melodic minor to incorporate the 6th and 7th scale degrees in your improvisation.
