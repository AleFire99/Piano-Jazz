    Pillar

    Scales Pillar

    The Scales Pillar forms the foundation of jazz piano practice, enhancing finger dexterity, improving your understanding of key relationships, and developing a strong sense of rhythm. This is essential for mastering jazz improvisation and harmony.

    Why Practice Scales?

    • Develop finger strength and control.
    • Build muscle memory for key relationships.
    • Lay the groundwork for improvisation and chord voicings.

    Scale Degrees Nomenclature

    Degree Name Description
    1 Tonic Key note of the scale
    2 Supertonic Second note
    3 Mediant Third note
    4 Subdominant Fourth note
    5 Dominant Fifth note, leads back to tonic
    6 Submediant Sixth note
    7 Subtonic Two semitones below tonic
    7 Leading Tone One semitone below tonic
    8 (1) Tonic (Octave) First note an octave higher

    Connecting Scales and Cadences

    As you progress through different scales, understanding how they interact with cadences is crucial for creating musical phrases that feel complete. Cadences are chord progressions that signal the end of a phrase, section, or piece of music. They help define the structure and resolution within your playing.

    Why Cadences Matter

    Cadences provide a sense of closure (or lack thereof) depending on their form. By practicing scales and understanding their relationship to cadences, you'll develop a deeper understanding of harmony and how chords resolve naturally within a key. While scales focus on individual notes and intervals, cadences focus on the relationships between chords and how they guide the music forward or conclude it.

    Types of Cadences

    There are two main categories of cadences: - Finished Cadences: These provide a sense of full resolution, always ending on the tonic (I). - Unfinished Cadences: These leave the phrase feeling open-ended and never end on the tonic (I).

    For more details on the various types of cadences and how to use them effectively in your music, check out the cadence document.

    Mastering scales will not only improve your technical abilities but also give you the confidence to explore jazz standards and improvisation. Start with C Major & Minor#Scales and work your way through the keys.
